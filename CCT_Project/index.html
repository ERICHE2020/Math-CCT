Reciprocal Function

Equation: y = 1/x

Transformations:
The basic reciprocal function y = 1/x
forms two separate curved branches,
one in the first quadrant and one in the third quadrant.
With no shifts or stretches,
it stays in its standard hyperbola shape,
symmetric about the lines y = x and y = -x.

Asymptotes:
Vertical asymptote: x = 0
Horizontal asymptote: y = 0

Domain & Range:
Domain: (-∞, 0) ∪ (0, +∞)
Range: (-∞, 0) ∪ (0, +∞)

Desmos:

Reciprocal Quadratic Function

Equation: y=-13/x²+1

Transformations:
From the parent function y = 1/x²
the graph is reflected across the x-axis,
vertically stretched by a factor of 13,
and translated upward by 1 unit.
There is no horizontal translation or stretch.

Asymptotes:
Vertical asymptote: x = 0
Horizontal asymptote: y = 1

Domain & Range:
Domain: x ∈ (-∞, 0) ∪ (0, +∞),
Range: y ∈ (-∞, 1)

Desmos:

Polynomial Function

Equation: y=0.5x²+0.26x³-0.97x⁴

Transformations:
This is a quartic polynomial based on the general shape of the parent function y = x⁴
Because the leading coefficient is negative (-0.97), the graph opens downward (it is reflected across the x-axis relative to y = x⁴) and is vertically stretched by a factor of 0.97.
The additional x³ and x² terms bend the graph and shift the locations of its turning points; they do not correspond to a single simple horizontal or vertical translation.

Domain & Range:
Domain：{x∈R｜-∞ ≤x ≤∞}
Range：{y∈R｜-∞ ≤y ≤0.11}

Secant Function

Equation: y = 2sec(0.5(x+0.3))-1

Transformations:
From the parent function y = sec(x)
the graph is vertically stretched by a factor of 2
horizontally stretched by a factor of 2
shifted left by 0.3 units
and shifted downward by 1 unit

Period, Phase, Vertical Shift:
Period: 4π
Phase shift: left 0.3
Vertical shift: down 1

Asymptotes:
Vertical asymptotes occur at x = π(2k + 1) - 0.3, k ∈ ℤ.

Domain & Range:
Domain: all real x except x = π(2k + 1) - 0.3
Range: y ∈ (-∞, -3] ∪ [1, +∞)

Sine Function

Equation:y=3sin(0.5(x+1))+0.3

Transformations:
From the parent function y = sin(x)
the graph is vertically stretched by a factor of 3,
horizontally stretched by a factor of 2,
shifted left by 1 unit,
and shifted upward by 0.3 units.

Period, Amplitude, Phase:
Amplitude: 3
Period: 4π
Phase shift: left 1
Vertical shift: up 0.3

Domain & Range:
Domain: (-∞, +∞)
Range: [-2.7, 3.3]

Exponential Function

Equation: y= 0.06x²

Transformations:
From the parent function y = x²
the graph is vertically compressed by a factor of 0.06
No reflection or translation.

Domain & Range:
Domain: (-∞, +∞)
Range: [0, +∞)

Logarithmic Function

Equation: y = log(x)4

Transformations:
From the parent function y = log(x),
the graph is translated upward by 4 units.
There is no reflection, no vertical or horizontal stretch, and no horizontal translation.

Asymptotes:
Vertical asymptote: x = 0

Domain & Range:
Domain: x ∈ (0, +∞)
Range: y ∈ (-∞, +∞)

Desmos:

Hyperbolic Cosine Function

Equation: y = cosh(0.45x)-1

Transformations:
From the parent function y = cosh(x)
the graph is horizontally stretched by a factor of approximately 2.22 (since the coefficient 0.45 slows the input),
and shifted downward by 1 unit.
There is no reflection and no vertical stretch.

Domain & Range:
Domain: (-∞, +∞)
Range: [0, +∞)

Piecewise Quadratic Function

Equation: y = ±0.1x²

Transformations:
The graph consists of two parabolas: one upward-opening (y = 0.1x²) and one downward-opening (y = -0.1x²). Both are symmetric about the y-axis and share the same vertex at the origin. Compared with y = ±x², the vertical stretch factor is 0.1, making the curves wider. There are no horizontal or vertical shifts.

Domain & Range:
The domain of both parabolas is all real numbers (-∞, +∞). The range of the upward parabola is y ≥ 0, while the range of the downward parabola is y ≤ 0. Together, they form a combined graph covering all y-values, positive and negative, except they meet at the origin.
